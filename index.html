Explanation of drought levels
The United States Drought Monitor classifies land into six categories. The categories are bases on the drought intensity. D4 is the category with the most intense drought and D0 the category with the least intense drought. If there is no drought than the label None is assigned. More information about the drought classification can be found on the States Drought Monitor website.
Here we list the six categories.
  • None: no drought
  • D0: abnormally dry
  • D1: moderate drought
  • D2: severe drought category
  • D3: extreme drought
  • D4: exceptional drought
The drought severity is computed by combining these categories. It is computed as follows:
severity = 0.2 * D0 + 0.4 * D1 + 0.6 * D2 + 0.8 * D3 + 1.0 * D4
D0 is the percentage of area that has as drought label D0. The resulting value is a number between 0 and 1. Where 1 is the highest drought intensity.
